The Histories

Herodotus. The Histories. Translated by Robin Waterfield. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1998.

Anyone who lives for a long time is bound to see and endure many thing he would rather avoid. I place the limit of a man's life at seventy years... So the sum total of all the days in seventy years is 26,250, but no two days bring events which are exactly the same. IT follows, Croesus, that human life is entirely a matter of chance. p.15
