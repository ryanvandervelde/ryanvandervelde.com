Poetics

Aristotle. Poetics. Translated by Anthony Kenny. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2013.

Learning is delightful not only to philosophers but to ordinary people as well, even though they have less capacity for it.

Aristole 2013, 20

The difference between a historian and a poet is... that the one relates what actually happened, and the other the kinds of events that would happen.

Aristole 2013, 28

For this reason poetry is more philisophical and more serious than history; poetry utters universal truths, history particular statements.

Aristole 2013, 28

For tragedy at its best the plot should be complex, not simple, and it should be representive of fearsome and pitable events.

Aristole 2013, 31

When plotting stories and putting them into words one should do one's best to visualize the events... This is why, in order to write tragic poetry, you must be either a genius who can adopt himself to anything, or a madman that lets himself get carried away.

Aristole 2013, 37
