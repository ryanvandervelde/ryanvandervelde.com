The Analects

Confucius. The Analects. Translated by Raymond Dawson. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1993.

A gentleman avoids seeking to satisfy his appetite to the full when he eats and avoids seeking comfort when he is at home... He may simply be said to be fond of learning.

Confucius 1993, 4

One does not worry about the fact that other people do not appreciate one. One worries about not appreciating other people.

Confucius 1993, 5

If you lead them by means of government and keep order among them by means of punishments, the people are without conscience in evading them. If you lead them by means of virtue and keep order among them by means of ritual, they have a conscience and moreover will submit.

Confucius 1993, 6

When you do not understand something, to recognize that you do not understand it-that is understanding.

Confucius 1993, 7

If you promote the crooked and set them above the straight, then the people will not be obedient.

Confucius 1993, 8

What is over and done with one does discuss, what has taken its course one does not complain about, and what is already past one does not critize.

Confucius 1993, 11

If I do not want others to inflict something on me, I also want to avoid inflicting on others.

Confucius 1993, 17

Riches and honours acquired by unrighteous means are to me like floating clouds.

Confucius 1993, 25

The gentleman is calm and peaceful; the small man is always emotional.

Confucius 1993, 27

It is not easy to find anyone who studies for three years but is not intent on a salary.

Confucius 1993, 29

I have never come across someone who admires virtue as much as he admires sexual attraction.

Confucius 1993, 33

When the stables caught fire the Master said: 'Did anyone get hurt?' He did not ask about the horses.

Confucius 1993, 37

If you yourself didn't desire these things, [theives] would not steal them even if they were rewarded.

Confucius 1993, 46

Avoid being impatient... If you are impatient, then you will not be thorough. If you notice minor advantages, then major tasks won't be accomplished.

Confucius 1993, 51

What the gentleman seeks in himself the small man seeks in others.

Confucius 1993, 62

If one commits an error and does not reform, this is what is meant by an error.

Confucius 1993, 63

Only the most intelligent and the most stupid do not change.

Confucius 1993, 73

If hateful things are seen in one at the age of forty, that is indeed how one will end up.

Confucius 1993,

If day by day one is aware of what one lacks, but month by month never forgets what one is capable of, one may definitely be said to be fond at learning.

Confucius 1993, 77
